Hello there!

I am Venkatesh Mullur, a recently graduated Robotics Engineer from Worcester Polytechnic Institute. My passion lies in harnessing the power of cutting-edge computer vision, deep learning, and artificial intelligence to bridge the gap between humans and machines. I am particularly interested in empowering robots to perceive, understand, and interact with the world just as effectively as humans do.

I am currently seeking full-time opportunities to leverage my skills and contribute to real-world applications of robotics. My experience as a Machine Vision Engineer at CogniAux Lab with Dr. Ali Yousefi allowed me to develop a multi-modal framework for analyzing human pose estimation in conditional environments using machine learning. This project highlights my ability to apply my expertise in computer vision and deep learning to impactful projects with the potential to improve people's lives.

My experience as a Perception Researcher under Dr. Berk Calli, involved applying computer vision and robotics to develop a real-time system for accurate keypoint detection and pose estimation on the Franka Panda Robot. This project encompassed tasks like enhancing point tracking and evaluating AI models for image inpainting. It exemplifies my ability to bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical applications to tackle real-world challenges in robotics.

Furthermore, I worked as an Image Processing, Computer Vision Intern at DigiM Solution, where I honed my skills in noise reduction, 3D reconstruction, and image segmentation.

I am eager to connect with individuals and organizations who share my passion for the future of robotics. Let's explore the possibilities of how we can collaborate to create a more intelligent and interactive world!
